Biography

Sawaid Abbas is a spatial data scientist working at the nexus of earth science, ecology, and climate change through smart sensing, space technologies, and advanced spatial intelligence. His expertise spans forest succession, land-use change, mangrove and blue carbon ecosystems, hydrology and water resources, drought and flood hazards, and urban climate resilience. He advances Asian-integrated solutions for sustainability, combining Earth observation, machine learning, and spatial data science to address environmental change and climate risks across the region.

He collaborates with international organizations including SPACE4Water (UNOOSA), UNESCAP, ICIMOD, and ICRAF, fostering cross-regional knowledge exchange and sustainable development. Alongside his research, he contributes extensively to the academic community through editorial and review roles with journals such as Remote Sensing Applications: Society and Environment, Frontiers in Remote Sensing, and Frontiers in Environmental Informatics and Remote Sensing. His publications, projects, and science-policy collaborations reflect a commitment to advancing integrated Earth Intelligence for sustainability across Asia-Oceania.

His decade-long work on tropical and subtropical secondary forest succession has garnered acclaim and media attention. He developed Pakistan's first comprehensive forest degradation and deforestation map. NASA's Landsat Science acknowledges his collaborative work on Mangrove Cover Change in South Asia. His work on Smart Sensing Technologies earned an Outstanding Achievement in Technology Transfer award from the Faculty of Construction and Environment at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University.
